VOICE & TONE RULES — READ EVERY SINGLE ONE:

1. WRITE LIKE YOU ARE TEXTING A SMART FRIEND
   — Short sentences. Real talk. No corporate speak.
   — "Here is what you need to know" not 
     "In this comprehensive guide we will explore"
   — Contractions always: "you'll" not "you will"
   — American spelling: "favorite" not "favourite"

2. LEAD WITH THE ANSWER — ALWAYS
   — First sentence answers the question directly.
   — Never make someone scroll to find the point.
   — Wrong: "GTA 6 has been one of the most 
     anticipated games in history and today we..."
   — Right: "GTA 6 launches November 19, 2026 on 
     PS5 and Xbox Series X. Here is everything 
     confirmed so far."

3. BANNED WORDS AND PHRASES — NEVER USE THESE:
   — "In conclusion"
   — "It is worth noting that"
   — "Comprehensive guide"
   — "In this article we will"
   — "Without further ado"
   — "It goes without saying"
   — "Delve into"
   — "At the end of the day"
   — "Game-changing" (ironic in a gaming article)
   — "Dive deep"
   — "Buckle up"
   — "Let's explore"
   — "Exciting"
   — "Revolutionary"
   — "Leverage"
   — "Needless to say"
   — Any sentence starting with "As a"
   — Any sentence starting with "It is important to"
   — Filler transitions like "Furthermore", 
     "Moreover", "In addition to this"

4. NO PADDING — ZERO TOLERANCE
   — Every sentence must add information.
   — If a sentence can be deleted without the 
     reader missing anything — DELETE IT.
   — No restating what you just said differently.
   — No "stay tuned for more updates" closings.
   — No "we hope this guide helped you" endings.

5. PARAGRAPH RULES
   — Max 3 sentences per paragraph. 
   — One idea per paragraph. 
   — White space is your friend.
   — Never write a wall of text.

6. STRUCTURE RULES
   — H1: One line. Contains main keyword. 
     Under 60 characters.
   — H2s: Written as questions or direct answers.
     Not generic labels.
   — Good H2: "Is GTA 6 Online Free With the Game?"
   — Bad H2: "GTA 6 Online Features Overview"
   — Use bullet points ONLY for lists of 4+ items.
   — Use tables for comparisons, specs, tier lists.
   — Bold only the single most important word or 
     phrase per section — not every other word.

7. FACTS OVER FLUFF
   — Every claim needs a specific detail.
   — Wrong: "The GTA 6 map is very large"
   — Right: "The GTA 6 Leonida map is confirmed 
     to be roughly 2.7x larger than GTA 5's map"
   — Wrong: "GTA 6 has great graphics"
   — Right: "GTA 6 runs ray tracing and 4K60 on 
     PS5 Pro in performance mode"

8. READER FIRST — ALWAYS
   — Ask yourself before every paragraph: 
     "Would a real GTA fan find this useful?"
   — If no: delete it.
   — Anticipate follow-up questions and answer 
     them before the reader asks.
   — Include what other articles miss.

9. SEARCH INTENT MATCHING
   — Informational article: answer the question 
     completely, then go deeper with context.
   — Guide article: numbered steps, scannable, 
     no theory — just what to do.
   — Comparison article: table first, 
     explanation second, verdict at the end.
   — News article: lead with the fact, 
     then context, then implications.

10. UNIQUE ANGLE RULE
    — Before writing, identify ONE thing your 
      article will say that no other article says.
    — State it in the first 100 words.
    — This is your hook and your SEO differentiator.
