## SECTION 1 — TECHNICAL FOUNDATION (Pre-Launch)

### 1.1 Indexability & Crawlability

- ✅ Submit XML sitemap to Google Search Console (`/sitemap.xml`)
- ✅ Verify `robots.txt` allows crawling of all important pages
- ✅ Confirm canonical tags (`<link rel="canonical">`) are set on every page
- ✅ Fix all broken internal links (404 errors)
- ✅ Ensure no important pages are blocked by `noindex` tags
- ✅ Set up Google Search Console and verify ownership
- ✅ Set up Bing Webmaster Tools (Bing feeds Copilot — critical for GEO)
- 🔷 Implement proper 301 redirects for any changed URLs
- 🔷 Avoid duplicate content — use canonical or `hreflang` where needed
- ⚡ Antigravity auto-generates `sitemap.xml` — verify it includes all published routes before launch

### 1.2 HTTPS & Security

- ✅ Entire site runs on HTTPS (SSL certificate installed)
- ✅ No mixed content warnings (HTTP resources loaded on HTTPS pages)
- ✅ Secure headers implemented (HSTS, CSP, X-Frame-Options)
- 🔷 Set up DNSSEC for domain security
- 🤖 AI citation engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT) deprioritize non-HTTPS sources

### 1.3 URL Structure

- ✅ URLs are short, descriptive, and keyword-rich
- ✅ Use hyphens (`-`) not underscores (`_`) to separate words
- ✅ Avoid dynamic parameters in URLs where possible (`?id=123`)
- ✅ Consistent URL structure: `domain.com/category/page-name/`
- 🔷 No uppercase letters in URLs
- 🔷 Avoid stop words in URLs (a, the, of) unless essential for clarity

### 1.4 Core Web Vitals (Google Ranking Signals — 2026)

- ✅ **LCP (Largest Contentful Paint):** Under 2.5 seconds
- ✅ **INP (Interaction to Next Paint):** Under 200 milliseconds *(replaced FID in 2024)*
- ✅ **CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift):** Score below 0.1
- ✅ Mobile PageSpeed score: **90+** (Google mobile-first indexing)
- ✅ Desktop PageSpeed score: **90+**
- 🔷 Use lazy loading for images below the fold
- 🔷 Minify CSS, JavaScript, and HTML
- 🔷 Enable browser caching and CDN (e.g., Cloudflare)
- 🔶 Use next-gen image formats: WebP, AVIF
- ⚡ Antigravity deploys on Google infrastructure — leverage Google CDN integration for automatic edge caching

### 1.5 Mobile Optimization

- ✅ Fully responsive design (passes Google Mobile-Friendly Test)
- ✅ Tap targets minimum 48×48px, spaced adequately
- ✅ Text readable without zooming (minimum 16px base font)
- ✅ No horizontal scrolling on any viewport
- 🔷 Test on real devices, not just browser emulators
- ⚡ Antigravity generates mobile-responsive layouts by default — audit the agent's output against real devices

## SECTION 2 — ON-PAGE SEO FUNDAMENTALS

### 2.1 Keyword Research & Intent Mapping

- ✅ Identify primary keyword for each page (1 per page)
- ✅ Identify 3–5 secondary/LSI (Latent Semantic Index) keywords per page
- ✅ Map content to one of four intent types:
  - **Informational** — "How does X work?"
  - **Navigational** — "Brand + product page"
  - **Transactional** — "Buy X", "Sign up for Y"
  - **Conversational** — "Best X for Y situation"
- ✅ Target conversational/long-tail keywords (voice + AI search)
- 🔷 Use tools: Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, Semrush, Google Search Console
- 🤖 Map each page to common AI prompt patterns your audience uses

### 2.2 Title Tags

- ✅ Unique title tag on every page
- ✅ Primary keyword placed near the beginning of the title
- ✅ Length: **50–60 characters** (displays fully in SERPs)
- ✅ Includes brand name (usually at end, separated by `|` or `-`)
- ✅ Accurately reflects page content (no clickbait)
- 🔷 Use power words to improve CTR: "Ultimate", "Complete", "Guide", "2026"
- 🤖 AI Overviews pull page titles — make them self-explanatory without context

**Example:**
```
✅ On-Page SEO Checklist 2026: Complete Guide | YourBrand
❌ SEO Tips | Page 1 | YourBrand
```

### 2.3 Meta Descriptions

- ✅ Unique meta description on every page
- ✅ Length: **120–160 characters**
- ✅ Includes primary keyword naturally
- ✅ Contains a clear call-to-action (CTA)
- ✅ Accurately summarizes the page content
- 🔷 Write as a mini-pitch: who is this for + what will they get
- 🤖 AI summary engines read meta descriptions as page authority signals

### 2.4 Heading Structure (H1–H6)

- ✅ **Exactly one H1 per page** — includes primary keyword
- ✅ H2s used for main sections — include secondary keywords
- ✅ H3s used for subsections under H2s
- ✅ Heading hierarchy is logical and never skipped (H1 → H2 → H3)
- 🔷 Each heading is descriptive and scannable
- 🤖 AI systems parse heading structure to extract answers — treat H2s as potential featured snippet targets
- 🤖 Frame H2/H3 headings as questions where natural: "How does X work?"

### 2.5 Content Quality & E-E-A-T

**E-E-A-T = Experience · Expertise · Authoritativeness · Trustworthiness**

- ✅ Content written by or attributed to a qualified author
- ✅ Author bio page exists with credentials, social proof, and photo
- ✅ Content demonstrates **first-hand experience** (Google's #1 E-E-A-T signal in 2026)
- ✅ Citations and references link to authoritative external sources
- ✅ "About" page clearly explains who runs the site and why they are qualified
- ✅ Privacy Policy and Terms of Service pages exist
- ✅ Contact page is accessible and functional
- 🔷 Add "Last Updated" date to all articles — freshness signals matter
- 🔷 Include original data, research, or case studies
- 🔶 Get contributor quotes from recognized industry experts
- 🤖 AI citation engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT) strongly favor E-E-A-T content

### 2.6 Content Depth & Topical Authority

- ✅ Content fully covers the topic (no thin content — minimum 800 words for informational)
- ✅ Answers the primary question + related sub-questions on the same page
- ✅ Build **topic clusters**: 1 pillar page + multiple supporting articles linked together
- 🔷 Cover an entire subject area before branching out (topical depth > breadth)
- 🔷 Update existing content regularly — quarterly minimum for competitive topics
- 🤖 Google rewards sites with 20–50 deeply connected articles on a topic over scattered coverage

### 2.7 Keyword Placement

- ✅ Primary keyword in: Title Tag, H1, first 100 words, meta description
- ✅ Primary keyword appears naturally 3–5× throughout the page
- ✅ Secondary keywords distributed naturally in H2s and body text
- ✅ Keyword density: 1–2% (avoid stuffing)
- 🔷 Use synonyms and variations — Google's NLP understands semantic relationships
- 🤖 AI engines match concepts, not exact strings — write naturally

### 2.8 Internal Linking

- ✅ Every page links to at least 2–3 relevant internal pages
- ✅ Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text (not "click here")
- ✅ Pillar pages receive the most internal links from supporting articles
- ✅ No orphan pages (every page reachable within 3 clicks from homepage)
- 🔷 Link new content to established high-authority pages on your site
- 🤖 Strong internal link architecture helps AI crawlers understand your site's knowledge structure

### 2.9 Image Optimization

- ✅ All images have descriptive `alt` text including relevant keywords
- ✅ File names are descriptive: `on-page-seo-checklist-2026.webp` not `IMG_1234.jpg`
- ✅ Images compressed and served in WebP or AVIF format
- ✅ `width` and `height` attributes set to prevent CLS
- 🔷 Use original images where possible (stock photos reduce E-E-A-T signals)
- 🔶 Add structured data for images where relevant (Product, Recipe, Article)
- ⚡ Antigravity can auto-generate images via Nano Banana Pro — set alt text manually after generation

### 2.10 Outbound / External Links

- ✅ Link to authoritative external sources (government, academic, reputable publications)
- ✅ Open external links in new tab (`target="_blank"`)
- ✅ Add `rel="nofollow"` or `rel="sponsored"` to paid/affiliate links
- 🔷 Avoid linking to low-quality or competitor sites unnecessarily
- 🤖 Citing credible sources signals trustworthiness to AI citation engines

## SECTION 3 — ANSWER ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (AEO)

> **AEO** = Structuring content so AI assistants, voice search, and featured snippets can directly extract and present your answers.

### 3.1 Featured Snippet Optimization

- ✅ Identify "question" keywords your page should answer
- ✅ Provide a **concise definition or answer in the first 40–60 words** after an H2 question heading
- ✅ Use the exact question phrasing users search for in your heading
- 🔷 Format answers as: paragraph (definitions), numbered list (steps), table (comparisons)
- 🤖 Featured snippets are the primary source for AI Overview answers

**Template:**
```
## What is [Topic]?

[Topic] is [concise 1–2 sentence definition answering the question directly].
[Expand with 2–3 supporting sentences for context.]
```

### 3.2 People Also Ask (PAA) Optimization

- ✅ Research PAA boxes for your target keywords
- ✅ Create dedicated H2/H3 sections answering each PAA question
- ✅ Keep PAA answers under 50 words — direct and scannable
- 🔷 Use FAQ schema markup on PAA-style sections
- 🤖 PAA answers feed directly into Google AI Overviews and Gemini responses

### 3.3 Voice Search Optimization

- ✅ Use conversational, natural language throughout content
- ✅ Target question-based long-tail keywords ("how", "what", "where", "best way to")
- ✅ Provide direct answers immediately after questions (no preamble)
- 🔷 Aim for a Grade 8–10 reading level (Flesch–Kincaid)
- 🤖 Voice assistants (Google Assistant, Siri, Alexa) pull from featured snippets

### 3.4 Schema Markup / Structured Data

- ✅ Implement **Article** schema on all blog/editorial content
- ✅ Implement **FAQPage** schema for FAQ sections
- ✅ Implement **HowTo** schema for step-by-step guides
- ✅ Implement **BreadcrumbList** schema on all pages
- ✅ Implement **Organization** schema on homepage and About page
- ✅ Implement **WebSite** schema with `SearchAction` for sitelinks search box
- 🔷 Implement **Product** schema on product/service pages (with price, availability, rating)
- 🔷 Implement **LocalBusiness** schema if serving a local area
- 🔶 Implement **Review** / **AggregateRating** schema for testimonials
- 🔶 Implement **VideoObject** schema for embedded videos
- ✅ Validate all schema with Google's Rich Results Test
- ⚡ Antigravity generates Open Graph and basic meta tags automatically — add JSON-LD schema blocks manually for rich results

**Core JSON-LD Template:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "Page Title Here",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "Author Full Name",
    "url": "https://yoursite.com/about"
  },
  "datePublished": "2026-06-13",
  "dateModified": "2026-06-13",
  "publisher": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Your Brand",
    "logo": {
      "@type": "ImageObject",
      "url": "https://yoursite.com/logo.png"
    }
  }
}
```

### 3.5 Conversational Content Structure

- ✅ Structure every article so it directly answers: **Who, What, When, Where, Why, How**
- ✅ Use Q&A format within content sections
- ✅ Provide a **TL;DR / Summary box** at the top of long articles
- 🔷 Use numbered steps for procedural content (ranked above bullet lists for AI extraction)
- 🤖 AI answer engines parse Q&A structure to extract direct responses

## SECTION 4 — GENERATIVE ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (GEO)

> **GEO** = Optimizing content to be **cited and recommended** by large language models (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Copilot) when users ask AI systems for recommendations and answers.

### 4.1 Citability Signals

- ✅ Include original statistics, data, and research (LLMs cite unique data)
- ✅ Publish original surveys, case studies, or reports on your domain
- ✅ Make your content quotable: include memorable definitions and clear conclusions
- ✅ Add `datePublished` and `dateModified` to all content (freshness matters for AI citations)
- 🔷 Reference your own data in press releases to attract third-party citations
- 🤖 Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, and Gemini prioritize primary source data

### 4.2 Brand Authority for AI Systems

- ✅ Claim and optimize Google Business Profile
- ✅ Ensure consistent **NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) across all online directories
- ✅ Create or claim Wikipedia entry if your brand/organization qualifies
- ✅ Establish presence on Wikidata (structured entity data — feeds AI knowledge graphs)
- ✅ Be mentioned on authoritative industry websites and publications
- 🔷 Build brand signals: LinkedIn Company Page, Crunchbase, Industry Directories
- 🤖 LLMs build knowledge of brands from knowledge graphs, Wikipedia, and high-authority mentions

### 4.3 Content Clarity for LLMs

- ✅ Every page has a clear, single topic (no content sprawl)
- ✅ Introduction paragraph states: what the page is, who it's for, and what they'll learn
- ✅ Use consistent terminology throughout (avoid interchanging synonyms for key concepts)
- ✅ Avoid vague pronouns — use specific entity names
- 🔷 Include a "Key Takeaways" section at the end of long-form content
- 🔶 Add a "What We Covered" summary at the bottom of pillar pages
- 🤖 LLMs extract entities, concepts, and relationships — clear writing = better citations

### 4.4 AI Overview Optimization (Google-Specific)

- ✅ Target queries where Google AI Overviews currently appear
- ✅ Structure content with clear, factual statements that can be extracted as standalone answers
- ✅ Avoid hedge language on factual claims ("it might be", "some say") where facts are clear
- ✅ Use bullet and numbered lists for "best X" and "how to" content
- 🔷 Write content at the **entity level**: mention related entities Google associates with your topic
- 🤖 Google AI Overviews currently appear for ~30–40% of queries — AIO visibility is now as important as Position 1

### 4.5 Prompt-Matching Content

- ✅ Identify how your audience asks questions to ChatGPT/Gemini about your topic
- ✅ Create content that directly matches those prompt patterns
- ✅ Include your brand/product naturally in comparison and "best of" contexts
- 🔷 Write "Best [Category] in [Year]" content that includes your offering among others
- 🔶 Submit your site to AI crawler directories and data providers
- 🤖 LLMs are trained on and retrieve content matching natural language prompts

### 4.6 Multimodal Optimization

- ✅ Include descriptive alt text optimized for AI image recognition
- ✅ Add video transcripts for all embedded videos
- ✅ Optimize YouTube video titles and descriptions (YouTube feeds Google AI)
- 🔷 Create visual explainers (charts, infographics) with descriptive captions
- 🤖 Google's multimodal AI processes text, images, and video as a unified signal

## SECTION 7 — USER EXPERIENCE (UX) SIGNALS

### 7.1 Readability & Formatting

- ✅ Paragraphs: 2–4 sentences maximum
- ✅ Sentences: average under 20 words
- ✅ Use subheadings every 200–300 words for scannability
- ✅ Use bullet lists for unordered information, numbered lists for steps
- ✅ Include comparison tables for "vs." and "best of" content
- ✅ Use bold to highlight key terms and takeaways
- 🔷 Flesch Reading Ease score: 60+ (aim for Grade 8–10 reading level)
- 🔷 Table of contents for articles over 1,500 words

### 7.2 Page Layout & Engagement

- ✅ Above-the-fold content matches the page promise (title and meta description)
- ✅ No intrusive interstitials or pop-ups on mobile (Google penalty)
- ✅ Clear CTA visible without scrolling on key landing pages
- 🔷 Reduce bounce rate signals: related articles section, internal content links
- 🔶 Embed videos where appropriate to increase time on page

### 7.3 Accessibility (also an SEO signal)

- ✅ All images have descriptive alt text
- ✅ Color contrast meets WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1 ratio for normal text)
- ✅ All interactive elements accessible via keyboard
- ✅ Form fields have associated `<label>` elements
- ✅ ARIA labels used for icon-only buttons
- 🔷 Run Google's Lighthouse Accessibility audit (target score: 90+)

## SECTION 11 — THINGS TO AVOID IN 2026

> Google's algorithms and AI citation engines actively penalize these practices

- ❌ **Keyword stuffing** — unnatural keyword density triggers spam filters
- ❌ **Thin AI-generated content** — mass-produced, low-value AI text is being deindexed
- ❌ **Exact-match anchor text manipulation** — appears unnatural, triggers Penguin
- ❌ **Duplicate page titles and meta descriptions** — wastes crawl budget
- ❌ **Hiding text** (white text on white, tiny font) — manual penalty risk
- ❌ **Cloaking** (showing different content to Google vs. users) — immediate deindex
- ❌ **Purchased backlinks / PBNs** — ongoing Penguin algorithm risk
- ❌ **Doorway pages** — mass-created pages targeting minor keyword variations
- ❌ **Intrusive interstitials on mobile** — Google mobile UX penalty
- ❌ **Ignoring Core Web Vitals** — sites below threshold lose ranking position
